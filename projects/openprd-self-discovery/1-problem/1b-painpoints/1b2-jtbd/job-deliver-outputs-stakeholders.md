# Job: Deliver Discovery Outputs That Stakeholders Actually Use

## Job Statement
> **"When** I've completed my analysis and need to present findings, **I want to** generate outputs in the exact format my organization expects, **so I can** influence decisions without spending hours reformatting.

## Job Context
- **Who:** All PM personas — format needs differ dramatically by segment
- **When:** After analysis is complete, before leadership review or team alignment
- **Where:** Export, presentation, delivery phases
- **Frequency:** Every discovery cycle (weekly to monthly depending on cadence)
- **Related Pain Clusters:** C2 — Output Format & Delivery Mismatch [Source: painpoint-mapping.md]
- **Pain Point IDs:** PP-16, PP-17, PP-18, PP-19, PP-20, PP-21, PP-22, PP-23, PP-29, PP-30, PP-31

## Job Dimensions

### Functional Jobs (What they DO)
- **F1:** Export findings in presentation-ready format (Slides, PDF, PowerPoint)
  - Evidence: PP-18 — "I can't present an HTML file in a leadership meeting" — 2-3 hours reformatting [Source: sarah-analysis.md, aisha-analysis.md]
- **F2:** Get actionable, prioritized recommendations (not filler language)
  - Evidence: PP-16 — "substantial improvement potential is just filler"; PP-17 — "Tell me HOW. Give me three concrete approaches" [Source: sarah-analysis.md]
- **F3:** Adapt output depth to their context — 1-page brief for founders, full report for enterprise
  - Evidence: PP-19 — "35 atomic pain points... WAY too much. I need a top-3 list"; PP-20 — system doesn't auto-simplify [Source: diego-analysis.md]
- **F4:** Output in organization-specific templates (Confluence, branded decks)
  - Evidence: PP-22 — "specific templates, specific page hierarchies, specific review workflows" [Source: aisha-analysis.md]
- **F5:** Get experiment briefs instead of Jira stories (for growth PMs)
  - Evidence: PP-23 — "outputs experiment briefs instead of Jira stories, you'd own a niche nobody else is serving" [Source: tom-analysis.md]

### Emotional Jobs (How they want to FEEL)
- **E1:** Feel confident presenting AI-generated findings to leadership
  - Evidence: PP-16 — generic language undermines credibility [Source: sarah-analysis.md]
- **E2:** Feel that the tool saved them time rather than adding work
  - Evidence: PP-30 — negative value-to-effort ratio for founders; PP-18 — hours spent reformatting [Source: diego-analysis.md, sarah-analysis.md]
- **E3:** Feel that outputs reflect their domain intelligence, not generic templates
  - Evidence: PP-29 — ~20% clustering misalignment; PP-21 — enterprise tone alienates founders [Source: aisha-analysis.md, diego-analysis.md]

### Social Jobs (How they want to be PERCEIVED)
- **S1:** Be seen as data-driven and rigorous by leadership
  - Evidence: PP-16, PP-17 — weak prioritization and generic solutions undermine credibility [Source: sarah-analysis.md]
- **S2:** Be perceived as efficient — not spending excessive time on formatting
  - Evidence: PP-18 — "40% of time reformatting" signals inefficiency to stakeholders [Source: sarah-analysis.md]

## Switching Triggers

### Functional Failures
- PM generates HTML export → leadership meeting requires PowerPoint → 3 hours of manual reformatting [PP-18]
- Solo founder runs full pipeline → gets 20-page report → "I will never read this" [PP-19]
- Growth PM needs experiment brief → gets Jira stories → has to rewrite [PP-23]

### Emotional Failures
- PM presents findings with generic language → loses credibility with VP [PP-16, PP-17]
- Founder spends 2 hours → output less useful than re-listening to recordings [PP-30]

### Context Changes
- Enterprise PM needs Confluence in company template → generic format rejected by team [PP-22]
- PM scales from 1 interview to 5 → output volume becomes unmanageable [PP-19]

## Job Chain
- **Upstream job:** Analyze Qualitative Data Deeply, Connect Quant+Qual Insights
- **Downstream job:** None (this is the delivery endpoint) — feeds into organizational decision-making
- **Competing jobs:** Manual synthesis in Google Slides, Dovetail exports, hire consultant
